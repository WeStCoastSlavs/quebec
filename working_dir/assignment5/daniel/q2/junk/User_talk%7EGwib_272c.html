[change] Thanks for the review

Thank you for the review you gave me. I believe that I have learned a good portion of what I needed to know in the past few weeks (ever since I started editing the English Wikipedia again). Believe it or not, but I believe that my getting an admin coach over on the English Wikipedia has actually helped to improve my knowledge of certain policies so that I understand them better than I have before. As to the fact that I need to improve my article writing ability, I can tell you this: Not one article that I have created in the past (near past, and far past) did I feel like I was wasting time creating it. We are here to create and build an encyclopedia. I see the Simple English Wikipedia as one that doesn't have a lot of articles, and therefore, I write stubs about topics that I am interested in. As to the fact that you think that I make mechanical edits: yes, I do think that some of my recent edits have been mechanical (general article help and cleanup, copyediting, and the stubs that I have been creating), but believe it or not, but every single one of the articles that I have created in the past few weeks is inherently notable. Rivers in themselves, I believe, are inherently notable.

I would love to write more information about the river stubs that I have been creating, but the truth is, there just isn't anymore information to write. That is how it is with some articles. You just can't add any more to them because you feel like you've written all that you can.

As for expanding articles, I usually reserve the expansion of an article for times when an IP address creates a really short stub about an article that I know has more information about them. A good example of this was the Bunker article. I expanded this article into a 5 or 6 kilobyte article after I saw that an IP address created only a single sentence for that article. Any other time, I believe, an article does not need it. Also, I am not very good at expanding an article that is already written. I am better at copyediting and making sure that page adheres to policy than I am at writing or expanding an article.

Sorry for the long message, Razorflame 23:13, 31 May 2008 (UTC)
