[change] RE:

"Please remember to add interwiki links to articles that you create. This is important because it helps editors from other Wikipedias navigate to our version of the article and vice versa."

What is a interwiki?

(are you able to give an example?)

✰Runningblader 01:52, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

Yes, I am most certainly able to give you an example. An interwiki is a link that allows a user that is viewing an article to check and read other articles from other language Wikipedias quickly without having to type it into their address bar.
They are usually placed at the very bottom of an article, and they use the language code to identify which Wikipedia it comes from. The language code is what you type in before the wikipedia.org in the address bar. For example, this Wikipedia's language code is simple. If I wanted to add an interwiki link from this Wikipedia on another Wikipedia, say, for example, the English Wikipedia, I would load the English Wikipedia's version of said article and add this to the bottom of the article (make sure to alphabatize them): [[simple:<insert article name here>]] You would replace the <insert article name here> with the name of the article.
For example, if I wanted to add an interwiki to the article Magnetism for the English Wikipedia, I would add this to the bottom of the article Magnetism: [[en:Magnetism]]
It's that simple. If you need more help, please let me know on my talk page. Cheers, Razorflame 01:58, 1 June 2008 (UTC)
If i get info on OGame off of English Wikipedia. Do do I add [[en:OGame]] at the bottom?

✰Runningblader 02:02, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

Yes, that's exactly right, however, you need not get information from that Wikipedia's article in order to put it onto an article. In fact, you can just put in onto an article if that article exists on that Wikipedia. Cheers, Razorflame 02:05, 1 June 2008 (UTC)
Very well, but isn't there bots that do the ones that are in a differnt language?

--✰Runningblader 02:09, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

There is. The only one that you should worry about is the English interwiki. So long as an article has at least 1 interwiki, the bot will be able to add the others to it. Cheers, Razorflame 02:11, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

[change] Mission

What did I mess up for the article mission?

✰Runningblader 02:11, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

You didn't mess anything up. In fact, you did everything right! The only thing that you didn't do correctly was the interwiki link. You got the basic idea down, but interwiki links (as far as I know), are case-sensitive. If an article exists on another Wikipedia, you need to make sure that you type in the name of the article the same way that it does at the top of the page. Also, when dealing with articles like Mission, it is a good idea to first check the English Wikipedia to see if it is a disambiguation page. A disambiguation page is a type of article that says all the different meanings for that word, and/or all of its different uses. The article that exists currently at Mission is closest to the English article en:Mission statement. Mission is a disambiguation page on the English Wikipedia, so it should also be a disambiguation page here. Cheers, Razorflame 02:17, 1 June 2008 (UTC)

Hello Razorflame, A document can be anything, such as the bill of your last shopping, a shopping list, a contract, a payment notice. A manuscript is something else. I don't know if you had (or even enjoyed) Latin at school, but manuscript comes from 'manu scribere (that is to say, write with the hand. Therefore, a manuscript was originally something handwritten. Later, when there was printing the word also got other meaninings. Looking at all the languages I know they either have Manuscript (in some form: Spanish and Portugue: manuscrito, French: manuscrit, Russian (no, I don't know that one, I can read Cyrillic though: Manuscript (trasliterated), Romanian (I don't know that one either): manuscris, German: Manuskript or Handschrift, Dutch Handschrift, Danish: Manuskript).

English (350 million native speakers, about the same number of second language people) Spanish (About 500 million native speakers, about 300 million second-language) and French (100 million native speakers) are among the languages most often learnt as a second language. They have words very similar to Manuscript.

So please don't tell me that manuscript is harder to understand than Document.--Eptalon (talk) 10:51, 7 June 2008 (UTC)
