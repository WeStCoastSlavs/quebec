Mount Everest

The Mount Everest is the largest and highest mountain in the world. Mount Everest is in the Himalayas. It is about 8,848 metres (29,029 feet) high. Its summit is on the border between Nepal and China. It is above the Death Zone (about 8,848 metres (29,029 feet) high), where the air is simply too thin to hold up a human being, so usually supplemental oxygen is used when climbing.
