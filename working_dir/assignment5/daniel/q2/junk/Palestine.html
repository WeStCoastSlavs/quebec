Palestine is a region in the Middle East, but not a country, in the same sense that Central America is a region but not a country. (Many other countries do call Palestine a country.)The most recent official use of the name was during the British Mandate between 1922 and 1948. Many people lived in different parts of Palestine, and built their civilisations. These included Cannanites, Philistines, Phoenicians and Jews. In ancient times, Jews created the Kingdom of Israel in parts of Palestine called Judea and Samaria. Judea was conquered by the Babylonians. But Jews returned and rebuilt their civilisation. Then, the Romans conquered the whole area and renamed it Palestine after one of Judea's ancient enemies, the Philistines. The region is called the Holy Lands, and is sacred for Muslims, Christans and Jews. The region called Palestine, today includes Israel, The West Bank and The Gaza Strip. Palestinians and Israelis are discussing creating a Palestinian country most likely to be named Palestine. This country will most likely be composed of Gaza Strip, the West Bank and East Jerusalem. (these places are sometimes called the Occupied Palestinian Territories).
