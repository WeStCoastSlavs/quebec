Spaghetti

From the Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia that anyone can change

For the similarly named movie type, see Spaghetti Western.
Spaghetti in a bowl

Spaghetti is an Italian type of pasta. It is made from wheat noodles which are boiled in water for a short time, usually 5 to 10 minutes. Before serving, a sauce is usually added to them. Spaghetti sauce is made of tomatoes, and sometimes with cheese, basil and oregano.

There is a legend (story) about how spaghetti came to Italy. In the legend, Marco Polo found spaghetti in China during his visit in 1295, and he took some back to Italy.

Today spaghetti is eaten everywhere. In the United States, there is a kind of spaghetti called "Alvaro's spaghetti" which is served with alfredo sauce. In some countries,like U.S.Aor Canada,meatballs are often in the spaghetti sauce.

Cooked spaghetti with tomato sauce and Parmesan cheese.

[change] About spaghetti

In China, spaghetti is often made by hand. In Italy, spaghetti is made with a machine so that large amounts can be made quickly. Italy is the biggest producer and consumer of spaghetti in the world[needs proving].

Spaghetti is called by other words when it has a different thickness: "spaghettini (n. 3)", "spaghetti (n. 5)", and "spaghettoni (n. 8)".
