Organ (music)

From the Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia that anyone can change

In music, an organ is a keyboard instrument which makes sound by forcing air through pipes. They are common instruments in churches, and sometimes appear in theatres, especially older movie theatres or cinemas.

Types of organs:

  • Pipe organ - the oldest type, sometimes powered by foot pedals
  • Mechanical organ - powered by a crank; may include a street organ or barrel organ (these types are sometimes called hurdy gurdies, but this is not accurate), or fairground organ
  • Electric organ - a later type, which uses an electric motor to move air
  • Electronic organ - the newest type, which uses electronics to simulate the sound of a pipe or electric organ
