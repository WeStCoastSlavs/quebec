[change] Events

  • January 30 - Adolf Hitler appointed Chancellor of Germany by President Paul von Hindenburg.
  • March 3 - Earthquake in Japan kills 3.000 people.
  • March 10 - Earthquake in Long Beach, California.
  • March 20 - Dachau, the first Nazi concentration camp, is completed
  • May 8 - Mahatma Gandhi begins a 21 day fast in protest for India.
  • September 26 - A Hurricane in Tampico, Mexico kills 1.000 people.
  • December 5 - Prohibition ends
  • The Toyota company starts in Japan.

[change] Births

  • February 18 - Yoko Ono, singer, artist, wife of John Lennon
  • April 7 - Wayne Rogers, actor
  • August 18 - Roman Polanski, film director
  • August 25 - Tom Skerritt, actor
  • October 10 - Jay Sebring, hairstylist
  • December 11 - Aquilino Pimentel, Jr.
  • December 23 - Akihito, Emperor of Japan

[change] Deaths

  • January 31 - John Galsworthy, British writer

[change] Nobel Prizes

  • Nobel Prize in Physics - Erwin Schrödinger, Paul Adrien Maurice Dirac
  • Medicine - Thomas Hunt Morgan
  • Literature - Ivan Alekseyevich Bunin, Russian writer
  • Peace - Sir Norman Angell, British lecturer, writer, and Member of Parliament for the Labour Party.
